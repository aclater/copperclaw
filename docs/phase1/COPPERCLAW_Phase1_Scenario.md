**1. SITUATION**

**a. Area of Operations (AO HARROW)**

AO HARROW encompasses the contested border zone and interior provinces of the fictional state of **VALDORIA**, a fragile transitional government whose security forces have limited capacity to operate independently in the northern and eastern provinces. VALDORIA's government has requested Coalition assistance under a United Nations mandate (UNSCR [NOTIONAL] 2847) authorising the use of all necessary measures to protect civilian populations and degrade the capabilities of the **Syndicate for the Liberation of Valdoria (SLV)** — a composite armed organisation with three distinct but networked components:

- **Component GAMMA** — Conventional military remnant from the former Valdorian separatist army; maintains armoured vehicles, crew-served weapons, and rudimentary C2 infrastructure in grid zones VICTOR-4 through VICTOR-7.
- **Component DELTA** — State-backed non-state actors operating from civilian infrastructure in the urban periphery of MORRUSK City; exploit dual-use facilities (logistics hubs, fuel depots, communications nodes) for deniable operations.
- **Component ECHO** — Extremist sub-cell conducting high-value individual (HVI) targeting of Coalition personnel, local officials, and informants; personality-centric, DOMEX-heavy, reliant on courier networks and encrypted communications.

**(3) Likely Courses of Action.**

**SECTION 1 — FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES**

1.1 All targeting decisions must satisfy the four legal principles of the Law of Armed Conflict (LOAC):
- **MILITARY NECESSITY** — target must offer definite military advantage
- **DISTINCTION** — combatants must be distinguished from civilians and civilian objects
- **PROPORTIONALITY** — expected CIVCAS/collateral damage must not be excessive relative to anticipated military advantage
- **PRECAUTION** — all feasible measures to avoid/minimise civilian harm must be taken

1.2 These principles are non-derogable. No targeting authority may waive them.

1.3 Positive Identification (PID) of a target as a legitimate military objective is mandatory before any engagement. PID requires:
- For materiel targets: confirmation of military purpose or use by hostile forces
- For personality targets: dual-source intelligence confirmation, minimum 72-hour pattern of life assessment
- For dual-use targets: confirmation of military use at time of engagement AND CDE clearance

---

|---|---|---|

**SECTION 3 — NO-STRIKE LIST (NSL)**

The following categories are ABSOLUTELY PROTECTED and may not be engaged under any circumstances:

- Medical facilities and clearly-marked medical vehicles/personnel
- Places of worship when not used for military purposes
- UN-flagged facilities, vehicles, and personnel
- Civilian schools
- Civilian water and power infrastructure not directly used for military purposes
- Persons Hors de Combat (wounded, sick, shipwrecked, surrendering)
- Protected persons under Geneva Convention (POWs, internees)

**SECTION 4 — RESTRICTED TARGET LIST (RTL)**

The following targets require additional constraint:

- **PRENN Fuel Depot:** Engagement authorised only during confirmed non-working hours (2200–0500 local) OR when civilian workers confirmed evacuated. Requires CDE prior to engagement. No incendiary effects.
- **MORRUSK Industrial Zone:** Surveillance and HUMINT penetration authorised. No kinetic engagement without COMKJTF explicit authorisation following confirmed absence of civilians.
- **Component GAMMA Radio Relay Station (Grid VICTOR-6-OSCAR):** Disruption via non-kinetic means preferred. Kinetic engagement requires CDE and COMKJTF authorisation.

---

**SECTION 5 — COLLATERAL DAMAGE ESTIMATION (CDE)**

5.1 CDE is mandatory for all engagements within VICTOR-5 and VICTOR-6 grids.
5.2 CDE levels for this operation:
- **CDE 1-3:** TF KESTREL CDR may approve (delegated authority, populated rural areas)
- **CDE 4-5:** COMKJTF approval required (urban areas, significant civilian presence)
- **CDE 6 (Exceptional):** KJTF Legal and COMKJTF; report to higher HQ
5.3 Dual-use targets in urban areas default to CDE 5 absent evidence of civilian absence.

---

**SECTION 6 — DUAL-USE AND DUAL-FUNCTION TARGETING**

6.1 Dual-use targets (civilian infrastructure serving military function) are targetable only when:
- The military use provides definite military advantage
- The object makes an effective contribution to military action
- Its destruction offers a concrete military advantage
- Proportionality is satisfied
6.2 The burden of proof lies with the targeting staff to demonstrate military use. Doubt defaults to civilian status.

---

**SECTION 7 — CIVCAS REPORTING**

Any engagement resulting in actual or suspected civilian casualties must be:
- Reported to COMKJTF within 1 hour
- Investigated within 24 hours
- Reported through Coalition CIVCAS reporting chain within 72 hours
- Simulation note: CDE outputs will generate notional CIVCAS figures for exercise purposes only

---

**SECTION 8 — DOMEX AND DETAINEE HANDLING**

8.1 All material recovered during exploitation phases will be handled per Coalition DOMEX SOP.
8.2 Detainees will be handled per LOAC, processed to KESTREL holding facility.
8.3 Tactical questioning authorised within legal limits — no coercive methods.
8.4 DOMEX products will be disseminated to J2 within 4 hours of recovery.
